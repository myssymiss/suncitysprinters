Coach Marrissa Cammisa

Head Coach ‚Ä¢ Founder

My Coaching Philosophy ‚ñæ

At Sun City Sprinters, I coach because I truly love track and field and believe in doing things the right way. I started this program to give athletes a safe and positive place to learn, build confidence, and grow strong habits especially in a time when many schools are stretched thin and teachers are balancing many responsibilities beyond athletics.

There is no shortage of information in sports today, but there is often a lack of structure and time to fully apply that information on the field and connect it to athlete health and long term development. My goal is to help bridge that gap by providing intentional, educated coaching that supports athletes in learning how to train correctly from the very beginning.

I focus on teaching strong basics, setting clear expectations, and training in ways that fit each athlete‚Äôs age and ability. I also ran track myself, so I understand what it feels like to train, compete, and work toward goals, and I bring that experience into every practice.

Sun City Sprinters is a nonprofit team that serves our community and helps give kids access to quality coaching in a positive environment. Our goal is to help athletes grow, build character, and love the sport not just win races.

I am a USATF Level 1 and NFHS Certified Track and Field Coach. These certifications help guide how I keep athletes safe, teach proper technique, and plan smart training.

Most importantly, I care deeply about every athlete on this team. I take coaching seriously and want every athlete to leave practice feeling proud, confident, and supported, both on the track and in life.

USATF Level 1 Track & Field Coach

Foundation coaching education

‚ñæ

What this course covered

  • Coaching ethics, athlete well being, and positive coaching leadership
  • Athlete safety and risk awareness on the track
  • Age appropriate training and long term athlete development
  • Sprints: mechanics, acceleration, max velocity basics, and race execution
  • Hurdles: rhythm, lead/trail basics, progressions, and safe technical teaching
  • High Jump: approach, take off, flight, and Using plyometrics, circle runs, and back overs to build muscle memory.¬†
  • Relays: exchange fundamentals, handoff communication, and team execution
  • Practice planning and season structure: purposeful sessions that build over time

Why it matters for your athlete

Your athlete is coached with a clear system: safe progressions, strong fundamentals, and consistent teaching especially in technical events like hurdles, relays, high long and triple jump, shot put and discus.

NFHS ‚Äî Coaching Track & Field

Fundamentals + teaching progressions

‚ñæ

What this course covered

  • Teaching fundamentals of running, jumping, and event basics
  • How to break skills into coachable steps (progressions + cues)
  • Spotting common form mistakes and correcting them safely

  • Sportsmanship, communication, and athlete expectations

Athletes learn clean technique early, so they build confidence and improve faster without bad habits that are hard to fix later.
